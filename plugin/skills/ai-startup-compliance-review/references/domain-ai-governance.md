# Domain: AI Governance

Use this file when a matter involves AI model behavior, algorithmic decisions, generative output, human oversight, safety testing, or AI impact assessment.

## Role Classification

- Provider: builds, fine-tunes, offers, or materially controls the AI system.
- Deployer: uses an AI system in business operations or user-facing products.
- Integrator: combines vendor AI with proprietary workflow, data, or UI.
- Vendor user: uses third-party AI with limited model control.

Role affects obligations and remediation. Do not assume vendor use eliminates responsibility.

## AI Risk Factors

- Output affects rights, prices, eligibility, employment, credit, health, education, public services, safety, or legal/financial decisions.
- Users may reasonably rely on output as expert advice.
- System generates media, identity-like content, or persuasive content.
- System can hallucinate, reveal confidential data, discriminate, or be abused.
- No human oversight, appeal path, logging, evaluation, or rollback.

## Review Controls

- AI system inventory.
- Purpose and use-case register.
- Model/vendor card or internal model factsheet.
- Evaluation plan: accuracy, hallucination, bias, jailbreak, privacy leakage, robustness.
- Human oversight and escalation path.
- User disclosure and limitation language.
- Incident logging and response.
- Change management for model, prompt, retrieval corpus, and thresholds.

## AI Impact Assessment Triggers

Recommend AIA-style review when:

- The system is user-facing and materially affects users.
- The system handles sensitive, minors, or large-scale personal data.
- The system is used in regulated or high-impact decisions.
- The company makes public claims about safety, accuracy, bias, or compliance.
- The system is launched across jurisdictions or through third-party distributors.
