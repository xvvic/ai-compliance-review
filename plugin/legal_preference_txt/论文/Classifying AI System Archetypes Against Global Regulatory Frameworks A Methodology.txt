Classifying AI System Archetypes Against Global
                                       Regulatory Frameworks: A Methodology
                                                                             Michael Clark
                                               Co-Founder, Standard Intelligence, Limited, Manchester, England
                                                            michael.clark@standardintelligence.com
                                              Standard Intelligence Research | Our Commitment to the Community



Abstract – AI governance compliance requires organisations to answer a deceptively simple question: which regulatory obligations
apply to this AI system, in this deployment context, in these jurisdictions? Answering that question at scale requires a principled
classification methodology. This paper presents such a methodology, developed and validated against a reference corpus of 900 AI
system archetypes mapped to 81 regulatory instruments across 30 jurisdictions. The methodology operates in four stages: functional
characterisation using an eight-dimension assessment rubric, risk-tier determination using a six-node sequential decision tree aligned
to EU AI Act Annex III and comparable international risk classifications, jurisdictional scope determination from territorial analysis,
and sector-layer identification for systems operating in regulated domains. The paper provides detailed rubrics for each stage, a
structured Article 6(3) carve-out assessment tool with scoring criteria, a confidence-scoring framework for classification outputs,
six worked classification examples, and an analysis of classification in dynamic contexts including system evolution and regulatory
change. The paper argues that classification is a continuous governance process, and that organisations without a formalised
      classification methodology will find systematic compliance planning structurally unachievable as AI deployments scale.
